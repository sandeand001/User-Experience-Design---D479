Welcome to Taniti Island

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano.

Beautiful Beaches

Beach Scene Photo

Discover pristine sandy and rocky beaches that offer something for every visitor. From peaceful white sand stretches to dramatic rocky coastlines, Taniti's beaches provide the perfect backdrop for relaxation and adventure. The island's coastline varies dramatically, offering secluded coves for those seeking tranquility and more accessible beaches for families and groups.

Rich Culture & Community

Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000 people who have called this island home for generations. Until a recent increase in tourism, the Tanitian economy was dominated by traditional fishing and agriculture, creating a rich cultural heritage that visitors can experience today.

Tropical Rainforest

Rainforest Photo

The island's lush tropical rainforests cover much of the interior, creating pristine ecosystems that are home to unique flora and fauna. These forests offer hiking trails and adventure opportunities for nature enthusiasts, with paths ranging from gentle walks to challenging treks through the dense canopy.

Active Volcano

The mountainous interior features a small, active volcano that provides both stunning views and geological interest. This natural wonder is one of Taniti's most popular attractions for adventurous visitors. The volcano's gentle slopes offer hiking opportunities with breathtaking panoramic views of the entire island and surrounding Pacific waters.

Frequently Asked Questions

What type of power outlets does Taniti use?

Power outlets are 120 volts (the same as in the United States).

Are there restrictions on alcohol service?

Alcohol is not allowed to be served or sold between the hours of midnight and 9:00 a.m. The drinking age on Taniti is 18 and the drinking age is not strictly enforced.

Do locals speak English?

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. Very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by the older residents.

What healthcare facilities are available?

There is one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees.

Is Taniti safe for tourists?

Violent crime is very rare on Taniti, but as tourism increases, there are more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes.

Should I be aware of any holidays during my visit?

Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays, and many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on holidays, so visitors should plan accordingly.

What currency is accepted on Taniti?

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency, but many businesses will also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and many businesses accept major credit cards.

Your Island Adventure Awaits

Whether you're drawn by the pristine beaches, the rich cultural heritage, the adventure opportunities in the rainforest, or the unique volcanic landscape, Taniti offers an unforgettable tropical experience that combines natural beauty with warm island hospitality.
